Rapid Cavity-Based Mid-Circuit Measurement and Feedforward in a Neutral Atom Array

This research achieves mid-circuit measurement and real-time feedforward in neutral atom arrays with a cycle time under 100 μs, significantly enhancing quantum information processing capabilities.

Paper: arXiv:2606.24869

arXiv:2606.24869 — Rapid Cavity-Based Mid-Circuit Measurement and Feedforward in a Neutral Atom Array. Lee, Tsai-Chen, Ho, Jacquelyn, Lu, Yue-Hui, Xiang, Tai et al..

Enhanced Measurement Speed

The study reduces the measurement-and-feedforward cycle time to below 100 μs, a substantial improvement over the previous benchmark of over 1 ms. This rapid cycle enables more efficient quantum state manipulation.

By coupling atomic qubits to a high-finesse optical cavity, the researchers can perform local light shifts that tune individual qubits out of resonance, preserving their coherence during measurements.

$$ T_{cycle} < 100 \, \mu s $$

Real-Time Feedback Mechanism

The implementation of real-time feedforward allows for immediate corrections to measurement-induced phase shifts. This adaptive circuit is crucial for optimal quantum state discrimination and conditional state preparation.

The approach demonstrates that coherence loss in unmeasured qubits remains minimal, with less than 2% degradation observed.

$$ C_{loss} < 2\% $$

Measurement Fidelity

The mid-circuit measurements achieved an infidelity of less than 1% for the four measured qubits. This high fidelity is essential for maintaining the integrity of quantum information during processing.

The use of Purcell enhancement in collecting emitted light from the selected qubit significantly boosts the measurement quality.

$$ I < 1\% $$

Implications for Quantum Processing

This method positions optical cavities as a viable solution for fast control in neutral-atom quantum systems. The advancements could lead to practical applications in quantum computing and quantum networks.

The ability to perform quick measurements and corrections opens new avenues for developing robust quantum algorithms.

Takeaway

The advancements in mid-circuit measurement and feedforward techniques represent a significant step forward in quantum information processing, enabling faster and more reliable operations.

  • Cycle time reduced to under 100 μs.
  • Measurement infidelity below 1%.
  • Coherence loss in unmeasured qubits kept under 2%.
  • Real-time feedback enhances quantum state preparation.
