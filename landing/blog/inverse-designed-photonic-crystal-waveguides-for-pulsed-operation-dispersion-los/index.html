This research demonstrates a method to design photonic crystal waveguides that significantly enhance bandwidth and reduce losses, improving their performance for optical applications.

Enhanced Design Efficiency

The new approach combines inverse design techniques with an efficient mode solver, reducing design computation time by over 100 times. This efficiency allows for rapid iteration and optimization of photonic crystal waveguides (PCWs).

Such a significant reduction in design time facilitates the exploration of various configurations and parameters, leading to improved designs that can meet specific application needs.

$$ T_{new} = \frac{T_{old}}{100} $$

Improved Bandwidth and Loss Reduction

The optimized PCWs exhibit up to an order of magnitude increase in bandwidth while achieving a reduction in losses by a factor of 4. This performance is crucial for applications requiring low-loss transmission and high data rates.

The ability to maintain constant-velocity slow light across a broader bandwidth enhances light-matter interactions, making these waveguides suitable for advanced photonic applications.

$$ B_{new} = 10 \times B_{old} $$
$$ L_{new} = \frac{L_{old}}{4} $$

Trade-offs and Applications

The study explores trade-offs between bandwidth, disorder-induced loss, group index, and dispersion. Understanding these relationships is essential for tailoring waveguides to specific applications.

As practical examples, the optimized designs can enhance Purcell effect for broadband applications and improve phase shifters for optical communications, demonstrating their versatility.

  • Optimized for broadband applications
  • Enhanced phase shifters for communication
  • Reduced complexity in design process
